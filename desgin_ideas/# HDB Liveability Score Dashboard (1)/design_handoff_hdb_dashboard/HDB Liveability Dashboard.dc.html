📖 Glossary & Metrics Guide Plain English explanations of everything in this dashboard
🗺️ What is this app?
This dashboard helps you decide whether a Singapore district is worth moving into if it has no MRT station nearby. It analyses real-time taxi data from LTA (Land Transport Authority) and gives each district a connectivity score from 0 to 100. Higher score = easier to get around without MRT.
🏆 District Connectivity Score (0–100)
The main score is calculated using this formula:
Score = Bus × 0.5 + Taxi × 0.3 − Friction × 0.2
Score
Meaning
75–100 ✅ Well connected
Comfortable without MRT
50–74 ⚠️ Moderate
Manageable with some planning
0–49 ❌ Poor
Transit friction is high
Why these weights?
• Buses get 50% because in non-MRT areas, buses are your main transport
• Taxis get 30% as a backup option
• Friction loses 20% to penalise areas where taxis are hard to get
🚕 Taxi Metrics
Live Taxis — what does this number mean?
The exact number of taxis available in the district at this moment. Comes directly from LTA's API, updated every 60 seconds.
Taxi Flux — what does +/− mean?
Flux = current taxi count minus the count 60 seconds ago. +N means more taxis entering (supply arriving). −N means taxis leaving (could mean high demand).
Friction Index — what does 0.000 mean?
Friction = Estimated Pickups ÷ Current Taxis. It measures how much demand is consuming the taxi supply.

0.0–0.2
Low demand — easy to get a taxi
0.2–0.5
Moderate — some wait time expected
0.5–1.0
High demand — taxis disappearing fast
🚌 Bus Metrics
Bus Frequency Score — how is this calculated?
Based on average time between buses (called headway) at all stops in the district.

🚨 Anomaly Alerts
🔴 LOW_TAX alert
Triggered when taxi count drops below mean − 2 standard deviations. In plain English: if there are usually 15 taxis and suddenly there are only 3 — something is wrong. Could be: sudden rain, major event nearby, peak hour demand spike.
🟡 HIGH_FLUX alert
Triggered when taxis suddenly surge or drop by 10+ in one minute. Could mean: concurrent model nearby (sudden surge of people needing taxis), peak hour starting (taxis fleeing one area fast), or rush hour starting.
🔵 BUS_GAP alert
Triggered when the average bus wait time exceeds 8 minutes. Could mean: bus breakdown on a major route, unusual traffic congestion, or service disruption.
